Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 09, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how LiftHit ("the App"), developed by Josef Sekava, handles information when You use the App, and explains Your privacy rights.

Interpretation and Definitions

Interpretation

Capitalized words have meanings defined below. These definitions apply whether the terms appear in singular or plural form.

Definitions

  • App refers to LiftHit, the software program provided by the Developer.
  • Developer (referred to as "We", "Us", or "Our") refers to Josef Sekava, Korunni 56, 120 00 Praha, Czech Republic.
  • Device means any device that can access the App such as a smartphone or tablet.
  • Personal Data means any information that relates to an identified or identifiable individual.
  • You means the individual accessing or using the App.

Data We Collect

Workout Data

LiftHit records only the numerical values You enter (e.g., exercise weights and repetitions). This data:

  • is stored exclusively on Your Device and in Your personal iCloud account,
  • is never transmitted to, accessed by, or stored on Our servers,
  • contains no personally identifiable information — it is purely anonymous numerical data.

We have no access to Your workout data at any time.

Crash and Error Reporting — Sentry

We use Sentry (Sentry, Inc., 45 Fremont Street, 8th Floor, San Francisco, CA 94105, USA) for crash and error reporting to improve app stability. When the App encounters an error, Sentry may automatically collect:

  • Device type and operating system version
  • App version
  • Error stack trace

This data is used solely for diagnosing and fixing technical issues. Sentry processes this data on Our behalf under a data processing agreement. You can review Sentry's privacy practices at https://sentry.io/privacy/.

We do not configure Sentry to collect any user-identifying information or workout content.

Data We Do Not Collect

We explicitly do not collect:

  • Names, email addresses, or any account information
  • Location data
  • Health or fitness data via Apple HealthKit or any other health framework
  • Payment information
  • Device contacts, photos, or camera data
  • Any other Personal Data

Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR)

For users in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, the legal basis for processing crash and diagnostic data via Sentry is Our legitimate interest (Article 6(1)(f) GDPR) in maintaining a stable and functional application. No other personal data processing takes place.

Your Rights Under GDPR

If You are located in the EEA, UK, or Switzerland, You have the following rights regarding any Personal Data We hold:

  • Right of access — You can request confirmation of whether We process Your Personal Data and obtain a copy.
  • Right to rectification — You can request correction of inaccurate data.
  • Right to erasure — You can request deletion of Your Personal Data.
  • Right to restriction — You can request that We restrict processing of Your data.
  • Right to data portability — You can request Your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to object — You can object to processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Right to withdraw consent — Where processing is based on consent, You may withdraw it at any time.

Because We hold no Personal Data about You beyond what Sentry collects for crash reporting, most of the above rights can be exercised directly by contacting Us (see below) or by contacting Sentry at https://sentry.io/privacy/.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. In the Czech Republic, the competent authority is:

Úřad pro ochranu osobních údajů (ÚOOÚ) Pplk. Sochora 27, 170 00 Praha 7
https://www.uoou.cz

Data Storage and Security

Your workout data never leaves Your Device and iCloud account. You are solely responsible for the security of Your iCloud account in accordance with Apple's terms and privacy policy.

Sentry crash data is transmitted securely over HTTPS and retained by Sentry in accordance with their data retention policies.

We retain no Personal Data on Our own systems.

Children's Privacy

The App is not directed to children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect Personal Data from anyone under 13. If You are a parent or guardian and believe Your child has provided Personal Data through the App, please contact Us and We will take steps to address the situation.
